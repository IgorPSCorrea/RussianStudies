Introduction to the Cyrillic Alphabet

One of the first steps in learning Russian is to become familiar with the Cyrillic alphabet. Unlike the Latin alphabet used in languages such as Portuguese and English, Russian uses the Cyrillic alphabet, which consists of 33 letters. Knowing these letters is essential to start reading and writing in Russian.

History and Origins

The Cyrillic alphabet was developed in the 9th century by two Byzantine missionary brothers, Saints Cyril and Methodius. They created a writing system for the Slavs that was more suitable for the phonetics of the Slavic languages. Initially, the alphabet they created was called Glagolitic, but it soon evolved into what we know today as the Cyrillic alphabet.

The 33 Letters of the Cyrillic Alphabet

The Cyrillic alphabet contains 33 letters, some of which are similar to the Latin alphabet letters, while others are completely different. Here they are, divided into vowels, consonants, and signs:
